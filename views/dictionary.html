Ranks
  • Recruit : to enlist as a member of an armed service. "Yet, you don't know how much you don't know."
  • Rookie : a person who has just started a job or activity and has little experience. "Now you know how much you don't know."
  • Private : an enlisted person of the lowest rank in the marine corps : one of the two lowest ranks in the army. "Congratulations, you have a rank."
  • Corporal : a noncommissioned officer ranking above a private in the army or above a lance corporal in the marine corps
  • Sergeant : a noncommissioned officer ranking in the army and marine corps above a corporal and below a staff sergeant
  • Lieutenant : an officer in the army, navy, or air force with a fairly low rank
  • Captain : a military leader : the commander of a unit or a body of troops : a person of importance or influence in a field : to be in charge of : to lead (a team)
  • Major : one that is superior in rank, importance, size, or performance : notable or conspicuous in effect or scope
  • Colonel
  • General
