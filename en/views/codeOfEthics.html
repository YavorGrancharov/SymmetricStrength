Code of Ethics and Professional Conduct

Code of Ethics
any personal trainer, hereafter referred to as "Trainer", must possess, achieve and maintain the following ideals
    Trainer:
  • acts with honesty and integrity
  • respects human rights
  • do not discredit, lower or affect the honor and dignity of a colleague, client, or other member of society. This principle does not prevent him from providing, in a professional manner, an honest and competent second or expert opinion about the actions of another trainer where appropriate
  • is diligent, responsible and determined
  • provides a competent decision in accordance with the requirements and constraints of the profession
  • provide only those services for which he is qualified or competent
  • is transparent and candid in all of his communications. Do not misrepresent in any manner his skills, training, professional credentials, identity or services
  • defines himself on the basis of his contribution to the profession without inwardly or openly denying all or part of his sources of information serving him as a starting point in deriving a new principle, conclusion or movement
  • builds professional self-esteem on the basis of added value and not on the basis of differentiation from another practitioner
  • recognizes that the self-regulation of the profession is a privilege and that he has a continuing responsibility to merit this privilege
  • conducts himself in a manner that merits the respect of society and the profession
  • engages in continuing education and professional development throughout his career
  • participates in the promotion of the profession through advocacy, research and maintenance of the highest possible standards of practice
  • keeps his professional commitments by integrating all of the listed principles into his daily practice
Professional Conduct
    Trainer:
  • respects the client’s dignity, needs and values
  • conforms to customer wishes, except in a situation outside the technical competence of the latter
  • respects human rights of the client
  • shall not discriminate in the provision of services to a client based on grounds of race, religion, ethnic or national origin, age, sex, sexual orientation, disability or any similar ground
  • shall not treat or attempt to treat a condition that he know or ought to know is beyond his expertise or competence
  • shall refer a client to a qualified health practitioner where he recognize or ought to recognize a condition that requires health services that he is not able to provide
  • shall not assess or treat a client without informed consent
  • shall assume full responsibility for all care he provides including supervising appropriately persons to whom he delegates duties
  • shall maintain the generally accepted standard of practice
  • shall maintain appropriate documentation for all clients
  • shall keep all client information confidential and shall not communicate such information to any person without the consent of the client or the client’s substitute decision maker except when required or permitted by law
  • shall disclose his fees to clients before providing services
  • shall not charge fees that are excessive for the services provided
  • shall not physically, emotionally or sexually abuse or harass a client or any other person
  • shall not practice while under the influence of a substance or while incapacitated
  • shall not discontinue professional services that are needed unless:
    1. the client terminates the service
    2. has a reasonable opportunity to obtain alternative services
    3. there are reasonable grounds to believe that the client has or will become abusive
  • shall not engage in conduct that is relevant to the practice and that would reasonably be regarded as, dishonourable or unprofessional
