Pull-Ups (body weight)

Test your one rep max on pull-ups following the standards below. Put your result in "Max reps" field for the progression to be calculated. When fourth set started to feel easy, retest max and adjust. Rest 90sec. between sets.

Standard:

1. Overhand grip, slightly wider than shoulder width
2. Contact with the bar everywhere bellow throat
3. Full extension on every rep
4. Don't kipp, swing or kick with your legs
5. Follow the rest intervals exactly
6. Consider every rep that do not meet the requirements as a technical failure

GO STRONG!

Dips (body weight)

Test your one rep max on dips following the standards below. Put your result in "Max reps" field for the progression to be calculated. When fourth set started to feel easy, retest max and adjust. Rest 90sec. between sets.

Standard:

1. Neutral grip. Choose parallel bar slightly wider than shoulder width
2. Go low enough so that your shoulders are passing the top of your elbows
3. Push your chests up and contract the back muscles. The reason is to avoid going down by rounding from your shoulders or upper back, that is not dip, the second reason it to stretch and thus use the chest muscles more. Properly performed dip is the best chest development exercise
4. Avoid bending at the hips, keep in mind that your hips must travel the same distance as your shoulders, in other words your whole body goes down and up as one peace
5. Don't kipp, swing or kick with your legs
6. Follow the rest intervals exactly
7. Consider every rep that do not meet the requirements as a technical failure

GO STRONG!
